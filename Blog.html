Birdwatching

Read time: 2 minutes

Recently, I obtained a pair of Viking Azura binoculars, so sparked my interest in birdwatching. After a preliminary test at my local park – where I saw some Coots, Moor Hens, Mallard Ducks, Tufted Ducks and even a Grey Heron, I journeyed farther afield, visiting Lyme Regis.

In Lyme Regis I saw lots of Black Headed Gulls and Herring Gulls, as well as a few other rarer birds: Common Chiffchaffs and Northern Wheatears.

Sadly, my visit to Lyme Regis was cut short, as my family and I discovered mice in the room of the hotel we were staying at (having been woken up at 1:00AM by the sounds of scampering feet. Although I do love all furry critters, I like mice and rats much more when they are outside in the woods, rather than under my bed…

I’m hoping to continue my birding ventures when I return to university and want to take advantage of the surrounding countryside and scenery.

If you want to support birds across the world, consider donating to the RSPB Website.

Author: Maya Miers

Baking

Read time: 5 minutes

After reading Ultra-Processed People by Chris van Tulleken, I decided not to buy supermarket bread but to try baking my own. On Chris’ recommendation, I bought cooking on a bootstrap by Jack Monroe, and started off with her basic white bread recipe. This did not go very well at first – when mixing the dough, I added way too much water, resulting in a very sticky mixture which required copious amounts of flour to fix. In the end it tasted alright, although a little bland. I did not add nearly enough salt.

The next time I made this bread, I used less water and half a tablespoon of salt. This loaf was much more successful.

I then decided to try making wholemeal bread – I replaced half the flour with wholemeal flour. The end result was much more flavoursome than the white loaves.

My next challenge was sourdough. I chose the BBC Good Food Sourdough Starter recipe and began. I fed my starter every day for 5 days (as the recipe stated), however on the 5th day, it still wasn’t ready. I fed it for another two days until finally a teaspoonful passed the float test – the natural yeast was active enough! It was time to make the loaf!

Although I felt like I followed the recipe to the letter, my loaf was remarkably wet, and I did not have much hope when I put it in the oven. What emerged an hour later was a lumpy bread monstrosity, which when cut revealed a cavernous inside. After this failed venture, I decided to put sourdough making on hold for a couple of weeks, and return to my good old brown loaves.

Drawing

Read time: 5 minutes

I’ve been drawing for as long as I can remember, although I started making a real effort when I was 11. Initially, I was heavily inspired by Draw with Jazza and Tamaytka, loving their cartoony yet semi-realistic styles.

My favourite medium to work in has shifted over the years, from pencils, to watercolour, but now seems to be settling into acrylic paint. I love how it feels to put it on a page, and the wide range of colours you can easily get from mixing colours (I find blending with pencils more challenging). I also love the vibrancy of the medium, and how opaque it is after just one layer.

One of my aims for the future is to improve at painting portraits, and to be able to draw complex poses from memory.
